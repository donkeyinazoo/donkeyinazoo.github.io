Today's Father Day

Well, yes today's 18th June, yes today's father's day. I see people, my friends, people whom I don't know, people whom I know sharing photos, quotes and write ups dedicated to their father about the hardships they have suffered, the pain they have endured just to make sure their family's all good and well, happy on their feet, dancing on the swirling cherish-able memory. Yeah, I know it for good, they pretty are the pretty memory that they are talking about. Well, I sit here, "where" is none of your business *smirk*, thinking about all the sacrifices made by a man to become a well deserved father, a responsible man called father, who is ready to take up responsibilities, like a man, for his family, for his love, for his children. It's very easy to say, come to a conclusion about there amount of effort they put in, but it's never any much easier than not noticing it better. I don't know, what makes me write this thing down today, on here, publicly, but I'm writing it down. Keep it in mind, this is based on situations, it may or might not happen, but all I say is what you need to think. Think about and to think about. Things happen, circumstances come up, situations which you need to tackle down, but only to no idea of how it's done. Well, even I don't know what are they and how is it tackled down. I'm starting to write this down after a long hiatus, I'll say, cause I have somewhat seeing it hard to recall what made me write this piece in the first place, well this is what it is. In this all time, I understood, life happens with all it's calamities even with all the precautions and care and sensitiveness that we hold, still love happens with happiness and sadness with its own benefits, with its own dis-benefits. A father is awesome, he's so many things for his child, for the good, in the good, in the bad, but what if things get too much complicated than what people think about, and what usually does happen. I started writing this down thinking I can totally nail the account and pull it off, but here I am work no words to spill over, no exquisite way to sum it down, for all I know this doesn't have a better way to say than this. Just hold on to what you got cause you may regret not wanting it later in life, so preserve the thing that you have got. That's all.
